## One Size Does Not Fit All

The biggest mistake new crypto investors make is copying someone else's portfolio without understanding their own risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals. A 22-year-old with disposable income should have a very different allocation than a 45-year-old investing retirement savings.

## Three Portfolio Archetypes

### The Conservative (Low Risk)

- **70% Bitcoin:** The most established, least volatile major crypto
- **20% Ethereum:** Strong fundamentals, institutional adoption
- **10% Stablecoins:** Yield-generating through lending protocols

Best for: Investors who want crypto exposure with minimal volatility. Expected drawdown: 30-50% in bear markets.

### The Balanced (Medium Risk)

- **40% Bitcoin**
- **25% Ethereum**
- **20% Large-cap altcoins** (SOL, AVAX, LINK)
- **10% DeFi blue chips** (AAVE, UNI)
- **5% Stablecoins**

Best for: Investors comfortable with higher volatility for potentially higher returns. Expected drawdown: 50-70% in bear markets.

### The Aggressive (High Risk)

- **25% Bitcoin**
- **20% Ethereum**
- **30% Mid-cap altcoins** (higher growth potential)
- **15% Small-cap gems** (early-stage projects)
- **10% DeFi/yield strategies**

Best for: Experienced investors with high risk tolerance and active management time. Expected drawdown: 70-90% in bear markets.

## Rebalancing

Set a rebalancing schedule — quarterly works well for most investors. When one position grows significantly beyond its target allocation, trim it back and redistribute.

## The Dollar-Cost Averaging Approach

Instead of trying to time the market, invest a fixed amount at regular intervals. This smooths out volatility and removes emotional decision-making from the equation.

## Position Sizing Within Your Portfolio

Even within your crypto allocation, never let a single altcoin position exceed 5-10% of your total portfolio. Concentration risk is the silent portfolio killer.
