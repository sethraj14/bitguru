## The Reality of Technical Analysis

Technical analysis (TA) is not magic. It's not a crystal ball. It's a framework for understanding market psychology through price and volume data. When used correctly alongside proper risk management, it becomes a powerful decision-making tool.

## Support and Resistance: The Foundation

Every other pattern builds on the concept of support and resistance:

- **Support:** A price level where buying pressure historically overcomes selling pressure
- **Resistance:** A price level where selling pressure historically overcomes buying pressure

The more times a level is tested, the more significant it becomes. When support breaks, it often becomes resistance, and vice versa.

## Three Patterns Worth Mastering

Instead of trying to learn every pattern in the textbook, focus on these three:

### 1. Breakouts from Consolidation

When price compresses into a tight range (low volatility), it's storing energy for a significant move. The breakout direction, confirmed by volume, gives you a high-probability entry.

### 2. Higher Lows at Resistance

When price makes higher lows approaching a resistance level, it signals increasing buying pressure. The eventual breakout above resistance tends to be powerful.

### 3. Volume Divergence

When price makes new highs but volume decreases, it signals weakening momentum. This divergence often precedes a reversal or at minimum a pullback.

## Indicators: Less Is More

Most traders use too many indicators, creating analysis paralysis. Stick to:

- **Volume:** The single most important confirming indicator
- **Moving Averages (20 and 200 EMA):** Trend direction and dynamic support/resistance
- **RSI:** Momentum and potential exhaustion points

## The Crypto-Specific Edge

Crypto markets have unique characteristics that make certain TA approaches more effective:

- **24/7 markets** mean no opening gaps — continuous price action
- **Higher retail participation** makes psychological levels (round numbers) more significant
- **On-chain data** provides a transparency layer that traditional markets lack
