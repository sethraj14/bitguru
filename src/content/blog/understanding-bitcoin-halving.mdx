## What Is a Bitcoin Halving?

A Bitcoin halving is a pre-programmed event in the Bitcoin protocol that reduces the block reward miners receive by 50%. This happens approximately every 210,000 blocks, or roughly every four years.

The most recent halving occurred in April 2024, reducing the block reward from 6.25 BTC to 3.125 BTC. This mechanism is central to Bitcoin's deflationary monetary policy.

## Why Halvings Matter

The halving directly impacts Bitcoin's supply dynamics. With fewer new coins entering circulation, the supply-side pressure decreases while demand can remain constant or increase.

### Historical Price Action

Each previous halving has been followed by a significant bull run, though past performance doesn't guarantee future results:

- **2012 Halving:** Price went from ~$12 to ~$1,100 within the following year
- **2016 Halving:** Price went from ~$650 to ~$20,000 within 18 months
- **2020 Halving:** Price went from ~$8,700 to ~$69,000 within 18 months

## The Stock-to-Flow Perspective

The stock-to-flow model measures scarcity by comparing existing supply (stock) to new production (flow). After each halving, Bitcoin's stock-to-flow ratio increases, making it comparatively scarcer than gold.

## What This Means for Your Strategy
