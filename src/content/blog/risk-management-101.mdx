## Why Risk Management Matters More Than Entry Signals

Here's a truth that most trading educators won't tell you: your entry strategy matters far less than your risk management. A mediocre entry with excellent risk management will outperform a perfect entry with no risk controls every single time.

## The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1-2% of your total portfolio on a single trade. This means if you have a $10,000 account, your maximum loss on any single trade should be $100-$200.

This rule ensures that even a string of 10 consecutive losing trades only draws down your account by 10-20% — painful but survivable.

## Position Sizing Framework

Your position size should be determined by your stop-loss distance, not by how much you want to buy:

**Position Size = (Account Risk) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)**

For example, if your account is $10,000, you're risking 1% ($100), and your stop-loss is 5% below entry, your position size would be $2,000.

## The Risk-Reward Ratio

Only take trades where the potential reward is at least 2x your risk. This means if you're risking $100, your target profit should be at least $200.

With a 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio, you only need to be right 34% of the time to break even. Most traders aim for a 40-50% win rate with this ratio.

## Stop-Loss Discipline

Set your stop-loss before entering a trade and never move it further away from your entry. Moving stops to avoid being stopped out is the number one account killer for retail traders.

## Portfolio Correlation

Don't put all your risk in correlated assets. If you're long BTC, ETH, and SOL simultaneously, you effectively have one large position in "crypto" — not three separate trades.
